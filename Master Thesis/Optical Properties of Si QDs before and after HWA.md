Silicon Quantum Dots: Impact of HWA on Photoluminescence and Blinking Dynamics

This file contains an MSc thesis project investigating the effects of High-Pressure Water Vapor Annealing (HWA) on the optical properties of Silicon Quantum Dots (SiQDs) using micro-photoluminescence characterization.

Overview:

1. Characterization of SiQD photoluminescence (PL) intensity and spectra
2. Analysis of blinking behavior, duty cycle, and ON/OFF frequency
3. Evaluation of HWA treatment at different pressure conditions
4. Investigation of surface passivation effects on quantum dot stability

Key Findings:

1. HWA improved duty cycle by 10–12%
2. ON/OFF blinking frequency decreased by 0.04 Hz
3. Surface passivation reduced defect-related recombination
4. Enhanced stability of the quantum dot ON-state after treatment

Techniques Used:

1. Micro-Photoluminescence (µ-PL)
2. ImageJ Image Processing
3. Spectral Analysis
4. Blinking Statistics
5. Silicon Quantum Dot Characterization

Learning Objective:

Understand how annealing-induced surface passivation influences the optical performance and stability of silicon quantum dots, with relevance to optoelectronics and quantum technologies.
