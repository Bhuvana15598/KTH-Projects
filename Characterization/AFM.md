Atomic Force Microscopy (AFM) Surface Characterization

This file contains an Atomic Force Microscopy (AFM) laboratory study focused on nanoscale surface characterization of semiconductor materials. The project investigates the influence of scanning parameters on image quality, surface roughness measurements, and feature resolution.

Overview:

1. Surface analysis using Atomic Force Microscopy (AFM)
2. Characterization of Si/SiGe and Si/Ni patterned wafers
3. Investigation of scan size, resolution, scan direction, and scan rate
4. Roughness, valley depth, and surface profile measurements
5. Comparison of amplitude and phase imaging modes

Key Findings:

1. Higher image resolution improves feature visibility and measurement accuracy.
2. Smaller scan areas provide more detailed surface characterization.
3. Lower scan rates enhance image quality by increasing data acquisition time.
4. Phase imaging effectively distinguishes different material regions.
5. Surface roughness measurements improved from ~41 nm to ~15 nm through optimized scanning conditions.
