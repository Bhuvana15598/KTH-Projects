This file contains a laboratory study on Scanning Electron Microscopy (SEM), focusing on image formation, resolution optimization, and material characterization of semiconductor devices.

Overview:

1. Investigation of semiconductor structures using SEM
2. Comparison of imaging at different acceleration voltages (1 kV, 5 kV, 10 kV)
3. Effect of working distance on image resolution
4. Sample tilting for dimensional measurements
5. Material characterization using Energy Dispersive X-ray Spectroscopy (EDS)

Key Findings:

1. Higher acceleration voltages improve image resolution and feature visibility.
2. Shorter working distances enhance image quality by increasing signal collection efficiency.
3. Sample tilting enables three-dimensional measurements of device structures.
4. EDS analysis successfully identified material composition in transistor regions and substrates.

   
Techniques & Instrumentation:

1. Scanning Electron Microscopy (SEM)
2. Secondary Electron (SE) Imaging
3. Backscattered Electron (BSE) Imaging
4. Energy Dispersive X-ray Spectroscopy (EDS)
5. Surface and Device Characterization

   
Learning Objective:

Understand the operating principles of SEM, optimize imaging parameters, and perform structural and compositional analysis of semiconductor devices at the micro- and nanoscale.
