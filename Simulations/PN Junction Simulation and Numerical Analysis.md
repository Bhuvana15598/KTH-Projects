PN Junction Simulation and Numerical Analysis

This simulation contains a semiconductor device simulation study focused on the electrical characteristics of a PN junction, including numerical discretization effects, validation against analytical models, and the influence of doping concentration ratios.

Overview:

1. PN junction electrostatic simulation
2. Analysis of electric field, potential, and depletion width
3. Comparison of even and odd discretization schemes
4. Validation of simulation results against theoretical calculations
5. Investigation of doping ratio effects on junction behavior

Key Findings:

1. Depletion width remained consistent across different mesh discretizations.
2. Simulated results closely matched analytical calculations.
3. Increasing the doping ratio shifted the electric field and potential profiles toward the heavily doped region.
4. Numerical convergence was achieved with minimal variation in junction parameters.

   
Technologies & Methods:

1. Semiconductor Device Simulation
2. PN Junction Modeling
3. Numerical Discretization Analysis
4. MATLAB-Based Simulation
5. Electrostatic Characterization
   
Parameters Studied:

1. Built-in Potential
2. Maximum Electric Field
3. Depletion Width
4. Doping Concentration Ratio
   
Learning Objective:

Understand the relationship between doping profiles, electric field distribution, and depletion region formation in semiconductor PN junctions while evaluating the accuracy of numerical simulation methods.
