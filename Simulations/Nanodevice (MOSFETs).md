Overview:

This project analyzes the impact of MOSFET gate-length scaling on device performance using Nano-CMOS simulation tools. The study evaluates threshold voltage roll-off, DIBL, sub-threshold swing, leakage current, and drive current across bulk and SOI MOSFET technologies.

Objectives:

1. Simulating bulk and SOI MOSFETs
2. Investigate short-channel effects in scaled MOSFETs.
3. Analyze threshold voltage roll-off and DIBL.
4. Study the influence of oxide thickness and supply voltage scaling.
5. Compare predictive compact-model simulations.
6. Validate simulations against measured SOI MOSFET data.

Key Findings:

1. Reducing gate length increases DIBL, leakage current (Ioff), and threshold voltage degradation.
2. Oxide thickness and supply voltage scaling improve performance but also increase leakage.
3. Compact Nano-CMOS models predict stronger short-channel effects at advanced nodes.
4. SOI MOSFETs exhibit significantly lower leakage current and reduced DIBL compared to aggressively scaled bulk devices.
   
Technologies & Tools:

1. Nano-CMOS Simulation Tool
2. MOSFET Device Modeling
3. SOI MOSFET Analysis
4. Semiconductor Device Scaling Studies
