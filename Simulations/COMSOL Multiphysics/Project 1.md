Heat Flow Simulation in SOI Wafers 

This simulation contains a COMSOL Multiphysics study of heat transport in a Silicon-on-Insulator (SOI) wafer under localized transistor heating.

Overview:

1. 2D and 3D thermal simulations of an SOI structure
2. Heat source modeling using a localized boundary heat input
3. Temperature distribution and heat-flow analysis
4. Comparison of fixed-temperature and convective boundary conditions
5. Parametric sweep to determine optimal simulation domain size
6. Investigation of lateral heat spreading in thick silicon layers

Key Findings:

1. Peak temperature reached approximately 336 K beneath the heat source.
2. Thermal solution converged for wafer widths above 42.5 µm.
3. Increasing the top silicon thickness promoted lateral heat transport.
4. 3D cylindrical simulations revealed symmetric radial heat flow.



Learning Objective:

Understand heat dissipation mechanisms in SOI-based semiconductor devices and evaluate how geometry and boundary conditions influence thermal behavior.
